Style Guide: The Power Suit

By AUGUSTUS Editorial | March 4, 2026

Fit Creates Authority

A power suit works because the fit is decisive. The shoulder should sit cleanly, the jacket should close without pulling, and the trousers should fall with a sharp line. Confidence starts with proportion, not excess decoration.

Choose The Right Cloth

Wool twill, high-twist wool, and subtle worsted fabrics hold structure while staying comfortable through a full day.

Control The Palette

Navy, charcoal, deep brown, and black carry authority. Add personality through a shirt, tie, pocket square, or polished shoes.

The Power Suit Checklist

  • Structured jacket with clean shoulders and balanced lapels
  • Trousers with a crisp crease and proper break
  • Dress shirt that fits the neck, shoulder, and cuff correctly
  • Leather shoes that match the formality of the suit

How To Wear It Without Looking Stiff

Keep the suit sharp, then soften the surrounding choices. A fine knit tie, open-collar shirt, or suede loafer can make the look feel modern while preserving presence. The goal is professional confidence with enough ease to move naturally.
